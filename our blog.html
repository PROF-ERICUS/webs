How I Built My Portfolio Website

Building a personal portfolio website is one of the best ways to showcase your skills and work. In this post, I’ll walk you through how I designed and coded my portfolio from scratch using HTML, CSS, and JavaScript.

I started with wireframing the layout to understand the flow of content. Then, I chose a clean, minimal design because it puts the focus on the projects themselves. I made sure it was fully responsive so it looks great on mobile, tablets, and desktops.

Some of the features I implemented include smooth scrolling, a fixed navigation bar, and animated project cards. These small touches make the site feel more dynamic and engaging.

5 Productivity Tips That Actually Work

We all want to be more productive, but sometimes it feels like there are too many distractions. Over the years, I’ve tried many productivity methods, and these five have worked wonders for me:

  • Time Blocking: Allocate specific hours for each task to stay focused.
  • Two-Minute Rule: If a task takes less than two minutes, do it immediately.
  • Single Tasking: Multitasking kills focus; work on one thing at a time.
  • Pomodoro Technique: 25 minutes of work followed by a 5-minute break.
  • Daily Reflection: Spend 10 minutes reviewing your day and planning tomorrow.

Implementing even one of these tips can drastically improve your efficiency.

The Future of Web Development

Web development is evolving at a rapid pace. From AI-driven design tools to advanced frameworks like Next.js and Svelte, the future is exciting for developers.

One major trend is the rise of serverless architectures, which make it easier to deploy scalable applications without managing servers. Another is the focus on accessibility and performance, as search engines now rank sites based on user experience metrics.

My advice for aspiring developers: keep learning, experiment with new tools, and never stop building projects.
